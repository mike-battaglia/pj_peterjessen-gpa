Otis Courtney just called me to ask me to contact the Governor after hearing
his asking the radio interviewer for answers to how to fund a new stadium
for the Twins. I could not get through although I did leave a voice mail
message for Keith Rawen of his staff.
In August I sent a 22 page letter to House Speaker Sviggum and Senate
Majority Leader Moe, along with 6 attachments, regarding how they could set
about solving the stadium issue, I outlined how to do so WITHOUT ANY NEW
TAXES. In the letter to Speaker Sviggum and President Moe, 10 ways are
listed, with verification, of which 5 are private pay formulas. My
particular model, "Generic Model" provides 8 ways of raising the funds and
40 ways in 26 categories for generating revenues, which could be used for
and by news stadiums for the Twins, Vikes, and Gophers, as well as by the
Metrodome. Please note: one of the ten refers to the proposal Carl Pohlad
received three years ago regarding how he could achieve a new stadium for
the Twins without having to go to the legislature. You can see that August
letter plus the six attachments on my web site which just went up last
Saturday, www.peterjessen-gpa.com My most recent take on the subject is in
the left hand column site list under "MN Stadium Games Continued".
Not knowing who to send this to, as there is, inexplicably, no "hot button"
related to stadiums, I am sending it to all on your page, as it effects
them, directly or indirectly, too. And, as my goal remains to be as open as
possible, I am also sending this to the legislative leaders and their staffs
to whom I've sent the earlier materials. This way everyone knows. I did
not send it to you earlier as the Governer's position was that this is up to
the legislature. However, on his radio show this morning, he asked how to
fund stadiums. Otis heard his comments, called me, and asked me to call the
Governor as we have the answer to his question. The items on my web site
answer the question.
The materials above were done for Otis Courtney of Edina, who developed the
www.vikingsstadium.com web page. He first asked me to do a proposal for the
49ers. Then he suggested that I recast it for the Vikings. When I was in
town last April I met with an individual who is involved in stadium
construction. He said all major league teams of all sports should use my
model. He asked me to put it into a generic form, which I did. That is the
version on my website.
I am from Portland, Oregon. I am in town this week. My cel is
503-913-3849. Otis and I would be glad to meet with the Governor or with
Keith Rawen, or with whoever else the Governor or Keith suggest we meet,
and, of course, with any of those on the legislative list which has received
all of these materials as well, as explained below.
However, as you can see from my site, when I put it up I still thought my
thesis of building a stadium each for the Twins, Vikings, and Gophers, and
keep the Metrodome profitable too was still possible. Just imagine the
jobs, the boost to the economy, the additional taxes for education and elder
care, etc., that such a project could bring to the state and serve as a
model for the nation, and make you big bucks in theh process. As I put up
my site I came across Jay Weiner's book "Stadium Games: Fifty Years of Big
League Greed and Bush League Boondoggles," giving the history of stadiums
going back to the 1950's. The book makes it clear that the "consensus" in
Minnesota is that only three teams can be supported, and that any choice
between the Twins and the Vikings = Twins. However, I still believed it
could all be overcome; However, after reading Sid Hartman's columns over
the last several days, I now believe "the fix" is in (see next paragraph)
for a Vikes departure, Twins in a remodelled Metrodome, and a new Gopher
stadium (unless the calibration of "the fix" has been too fine tuned and the
Twins leave town too).
My own take is this: the previous owners of the Vikings were unwilling to
use their pocket change to make a stadium deal happen (and most of the ten
hadn't paid a dime for the team, just as Carl Pohlad didn't pay a dime for
the Twins, as each set used loans for which game revenues are used to pay
the debt service). NFL and Vikings testimony in the mid-90's said that
without a new stadium, the team could not be competitive, and they would
have to move. So these rich guys sold an out of towner willing to go
forward (Red loves to own teams). They knew that if the legislatuare
wouldn't come up with the money, and he had to move, he would get the heat,
not them. Very clever. The Twins would then get a remodelled Metrodome
after the Vikings leave, and the Gophers would get a new stadium and no
longer have to compete with the Vikes for the loyalty of fans. That is the
end game, the "understanding" of the players, in my view. I'm open to a
different theory, but these kinds of things just don't happen.
Carl Pohlad, obviously, as any neurologist could tell you, minimally has
senility plaque short circuiting his brain or some kind of dementia caused
either by TIA's or small strokes. I took care of my aged (80's) parents in
similar conditions (17 months with my dad until he passed and 8 years with
my mom until she passed in January of this year). I''m well versed in long
term care, multi-enfarc dementia, walkers, bed ridden, etc. It appears no
one is willing to take over from him, although Bud Selig, who the paper says
talks to Carl weekly, obviously has figured it out and is playing Carl, and
the Twin City press, like a violin.
The ill-considered 1986 Federal law (also discussed in a separate piece on
my web page, also sent to the legislature et al), is onen of the culprits.
However, ownershp of the stadiums or joint ownership would obviate it and
allow revenues to pay the bonds used to cover the amount not raised
privately (ALTHOUGH my model suggests that if a Dreamworks model is used,
that won't be necessary).
Please note, also supporting my thesis about the "understanding" of the
players, who are all positioning themselves to wash their hands of the
Vikings leaving town, is that NO ONE from the House or Senate, neither
elected leader or staff, has contacted me. No one. I work by clues. That
is a big one. What clue, in this regard, will I get from the Governor?
